Key Takeaways from Q3 2024

16th - 22nd September Key Takeaways:

  • Fed announces 50bps rate cut at FOMC
  • Political move to support market interest and avoid recession fears, going into Sep OPEX (30th Sep)

23rd - 29th September Key Takeaways:

  • China stimulus announcement on Tuesday 24th Sep: China unveils broad stimulus measures
  • Market sentiment in China is generally bearish. New monetary policy causes lots of unwinding and squeezes.
  • Hang Seng Index rallies the most since 2008
  • Chinese like luxury goods, LVMH surges
  • Nasdaq underperforms as investors sell it to bid on Chinese stocks
  • “Window dressing”: money managers make sure to be in outperforming stocks at end of quarter to show good books to their clients
  • Neighbouring Japanese markets see interest due to China stimulus, UNTIL unexpected new PM Ishiga is elected (hawkish policies)
  • Investors sell Nikkei, strong Yen bad for USD
  • China cut causes surge in commodity prices, EXCEPT OIL
  • Why not oil? More supply hitting market = price stays down
  • JPMorgan Collar trade keeps market pinned until OPEX.
  • PCE comes in cold (0.1% vs 0.2%) – Good for the market.
  • CPI in France and Spain came in much colder (1.2% vs 1.6%) indicating economic slowdown, suggesting deflation.

Key Takeaways from Q4 2024

30th Sep - 6th Oct Key Takeaways:

  • China in Golden Week holiday. Markets had generational gains right before.
  • Nike earnings used to gauge consumer inflation.
  • Oil surges after Iran attacks Israel, market prices odds of escalation in the Middle East.
  • ISM Manufacturing below 50, indicating contraction.
  • ADP Employment comes in hot – good for the market.
  • JPY/USD dumping as new PM gives more hawkish talks.
  • This week, the market is more volatile, lacks supportive flows from Sep 30th expiry. Vanna and Charm back next week.
  • HSI continues to outperform as money rotates into Chinese markets from Mag7 and Nasdaq.
  • JOLTS hot print suggests economic growth, higher inflation expectations, and rising bond yields.
  • NFP comes in super hot, Gold dips while SPX and BTC spike and retrace.
  • Gold is correlated to bonds – expansion leads to bonds and gold going down.
  • Port workers' drama solved with wage increases.
  • Iran and Israel tensions remain high.
  • YEN weakening, Nikkei surging as Ishiba turns dovish.
  • Global rate cut cycle in full swing.

7th Oct - 12th Oct Key Takeaways:

  • China retracing is good for the US market but affects energy and commodity prices.
  • FOMC minutes confirm the Fed's confidence in fighting inflation.
  • Corporate buybacks next week, but data-dependent.
  • CPI prints 0.3% (vs 0.3% previous, 0.2% expected).
  • PPI prints unchanged (0%), easing inflation fears.
  • Trump leading in election polls, supportive for markets.
  • Oil down, Trump basket up, BTC positively correlated.
  • Bank earnings: JPM reported $12B in earnings.

14th Oct - 18th Oct Key Takeaways:

  • Hedge funds chase potential rally into elections, with some hedging due to retail sales print, VIX expiration, and OPEX.
  • Eyes on TSMC earnings (indicator for tech growth) and retail sales.
  • BTC ETFs see $280-400M in inflows ($IBIT).
